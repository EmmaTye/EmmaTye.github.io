I am a computer science PhD student at the University of Strathclyde in the [MSP research group](https://msp.cis.strath.ac.uk/) in Glasgow, underneath the supervision of Bob Atkey and Guillaume Allais. Currently working on trying to make dependently typed programming more accessible by providing custom low-level representations of data types in an erganomic way.

Previously, I worked in London for 5 years as a software engineer, at a couple of fintechs using functional programming - Clojure and Ocaml. Definitely prefer OCaml - nothing can beat a static type system - but have to admit that the row-typing of clojure's specs has its uses.

In my day-to-day, I use Haskell/Agda for general coding/proofs respectively.
